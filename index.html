HE BUILT 7 AIS ALONE.And nobody saw it coming.

Dr. Carlos Montoya Bacigalupo spent eighteen months building HUMANA.AI — seven clinical AI applications — in near-total silence. No investors. No press releases. Just a surgeon with thirty years of documented cases and a problem that wouldn't let him sleep.

By WIRED Dental Editorial· Santiago, Chile· March 6, 2026
Dr. Carlos Montoya B.
Periodontist · Implantologist · Director, HUMANA.AI · Clínica Miró, Santiago
30 YRS
Clinical practice

There's a particular kind of professional who doesn't announce what they're building until the building is done. Dr. Carlos Montoya Bacigalupo is that kind. For eighteen months, while the dental AI space filled with funded startups and conference demos, a surgeon in Providencia, Santiago was writing code.

The result is HUMANA.AI — seven clinical applications covering implant risk, radiographic analysis, caries prevention, facial aesthetics, treatment retention, and clinical decision support. All in production. All validated. All built by one person.

HUMANA.AI · humanaia.cl · Launched March 6, 2026 · 7 applications · 1 vision

"Chaos didn't destroy me. It showed me what I had to build."

— Dr. Carlos Montoya Bacigalupo
You built seven AI systems in 18 months. Alone. Why didn't anyone know?

Because announcing something before it exists is theater. I'd seen too many dental AI products that were demos wrapped in investment decks. My rule: nothing ships until it works with real patients and I can defend every decision scientifically. So yes — total silence. When you have thirty years of documented cases and understand the clinical problems from inside, you don't need to ask permission to solve them.

ImplantX. 0.894 ROC-AUC. 4,126 cases. What's the actual methodology?

Causal AI, not correlation. Most risk models say "patients with these features had worse outcomes." Useful but incomplete. I used Structural Causal Models to map actual causal pathways — bone quality, systemic factors, surgical technique, patient anatomy. The model tells you which specific factor is driving this patient's risk. That changes the clinical conversation entirely. You're not reading a score. You're reading a mechanism.

Índice Miró: IM = M × E × C — Beauty is contextual, not universal. Morphology × Expression × Context.
The Miró Index rejects the golden ratio. That's a big claim.

It's supported by 5,500 faces across cultural contexts. The conclusion is consistent: beauty is not a fixed proportion. It's a relationship between morphology, expression, and cultural context. The golden ratio is 16th-century aesthetic theory masquerading as science. We don't use 16th-century surgical instruments — we shouldn't use 16th-century aesthetic frameworks either.

Success in three years looks like what?

A dentist in rural Chile — or Peru, or Colombia — accessing the same diagnostic intelligence I've built over thirty years, through a browser, for a fraction of the cost of a specialist consultation. That's democratization of clinical knowledge. It's technically possible right now. The distribution problem is solved. That's the version I'm working toward.

"A rural dentist in Colombia should access what I built over 30 years. Through a browser. That's the mission."

— On HUMANA.AI's reach in Latin America
